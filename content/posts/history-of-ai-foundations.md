Thesis: AI history oscillates between “handcraft intelligence with symbols” and “let statistics discover structure.” Periods of overconfidence ended in winters; scale, data, and compute reignited progress.

Milestones (very short version):
- 1940s–50s: Turing, McCulloch–Pitts, cybernetics; perceptrons (Rosenblatt)
- 1960s–70s: Symbolic AI, search, expert systems; Minsky & Papert critique kills early neural hype
- 1980s–90s: Backprop resurges (Rumelhart, Hinton, Williams), CNNs (LeCun), RL foundations
- 2000s: Kernels, SVMs, probabilistic models; GPU compute rises
- 2012–2016: Deep learning breakthrough: AlexNet, seq2seq, attention
- 2017–2020: Transformers, scaling laws, transfer learning
- 2021–present: Multimodal models, retrieval, tool use, agents, safety research

What changed:
- Compute density + parallelism (GPUs/TPUs)
- Data availability + self-supervision
- Architectural innovations (attention, normalization, residuals)
- Training craft (optimizers, schedulers, regularization)

Open questions:
- Data efficiency vs scale: how far can we go with less?
- Alignment and control: reliably steerable systems under distribution shift
- Tool-use and memory: from chat to competent, long-lived agents
