Every tool is a trade: we gain capability and lose a little necessity to practice. Maps shrink our need to memorize terrain; calculators diminish arithmetic fluency; assistants shift our cognitive load from recall to judgment.

The question isn’t “does tech make us worse?” but “which human capacities do we want to amplify, and which are safe to outsource?” My current bet: amplify curiosity, reasoning, collaboration, and taste; outsource rote recall, repetitive drafting, and low-level system glue.

Design principles:
- Keep humans in the loop where values are contested
- Instrument for feedback so mistakes teach quickly
- Build for graceful degradation: no single points of cognitive failure

Education will need a renovation: less regurgitation, more projects, critique, and synthesis.
