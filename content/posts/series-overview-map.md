This is my working syllabus—subject to change as I learn.

Series (tentative order):

1) Human History in Three Accelerations
- Energy capture → surplus → cities
- Writing/markets/law → large-scale coordination
- Industry → scientific method, fossil fuels, computation

2) Culture as a Cognitive Technology
- Myths, rituals, art: compressing values into transmissible form
- Memes and media ecosystems

3) Governance: How We Coordinate at Scale
- City-states → empires → nation-states → networks
- Trade-offs: liberty, equality, resilience, competence

4) The Long Arc of Technology
- Tools that amplify bodies → tools that amplify minds
- Software EATS coordination costs

5) How Technology Shapes Humans
- Attention, incentives, skill atrophy vs augmentation
- Education models in the age of assistants

6) The History of AI and Neural Networks
- From logic to perceptrons, backprop, transformers, multimodal systems
- Why scale works (and where it doesn’t)

7) Esoteric Maps and Transformations
- Alchemy, Kabbalah, Vajrayana, Hermeticism as symbolic operating systems
- Caution: poetry ≠ physics, yet metaphors can be instrumentally true

8) Reality and Time
- Relativity, quantum theory, thermodynamics, computation as physics
- Emergence, causality, and the trap of reification

9) Quantum Physics and Consciousness
- What we can say responsibly vs category errors to avoid
- Decoherence, measurement, and observer talk—what’s metaphor, what’s math

10) Language as a Technology of Thought
- How linguistic structures shape affordances and bias
- New linguistic prosthetics: prompts, programs, and protocols

11) Futures
- Plausible paths: abundant compute, abundant energy, abundant attention
- Risks: centralization, misalignment, epistemic fracture
- Institutions that could help: civic stacks, open science, alignment ecosystems

I’ll cite sources where I draw directly on research and keep each essay self-contained.
